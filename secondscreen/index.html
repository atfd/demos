The Concept Of
Second Screen Experiences

This demo showcases the implementation of a second screen experience with node.js and socket.io. After opening the site on two devices, they can connect and exchange information. There's a huge variety of different use cases for second screen applications, including, but not limited to:

  • Displaying Additional Information

    Example: While playing a computer game, the second screen displays a realtime map to the player, helping with navigation inside the game.

  • Access To Social Content

    Example: While watching a presidential debate, the second screen could display relevant social media streams and allow the user to participate in a conversation about the debate (e.g. twitter, real time voting, etc.)

  • Makeshift Input Device

    Example: Multiple users in an airport lobby can use their mobile devices to connect to a website that is displayed on a big screen. They then can use their mobile devices as game controllers and play a game against each other on the big screen, while they wait for their flight.
